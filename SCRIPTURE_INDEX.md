# Scripture Index

## Catholic Veritas

This document is the master Scripture index for the Catholic Veritas knowledge base. It serves as the central biblical reference framework used by all future contributors, whether human or AI, when researching or writing articles.

This is not a Bible commentary and it is not a concordance. It is an apologetics-oriented Scripture index that organizes biblical passages according to doctrine, theology, Church teaching, history, and recurring debates. Its purpose is to help contributors quickly locate the strongest biblical evidence relevant to a topic and to support consistent, evidence-based research across the entire project.

This document complements, but does not replace, the Bible itself. It is designed to assist research, article planning, and theological organization by grouping passages according to their relevance within the Catholic knowledge base.

## Introduction

The purpose of this index is to organize Scripture by theological and apologetics relevance rather than simply by biblical book or chapter sequence.

Catholic Veritas requires a Scripture reference system that helps contributors find:

- primary biblical evidence for doctrine
- supporting biblical evidence for explanation or apologetics
- contextual passages that prevent misuse or oversimplification
- related doctrinal and thematic connections across the canon

This index should serve as a long-term research tool. It should help contributors identify where Scripture speaks most directly, where supporting evidence strengthens a case, and where surrounding context must be considered before a passage is used in argument.

## Index Structure

Each indexed topic should eventually contain the following fields:

- Primary passages
- Supporting passages
- Important context
- Related topics

### Primary passages

These are the principal passages most directly relevant to the doctrine, theme, or apologetics question.

### Supporting passages

These passages reinforce, illuminate, or broaden the primary biblical case without always stating the point as directly.

### Important context

This section should identify surrounding chapters, literary setting, covenantal background, historical context, or interpretive issues necessary for responsible use of the indexed passages.

### Related topics

This section should identify other doctrines, themes, or debates to which the same passages may also belong.

The long-term goal is not simply to collect verses, but to create a structured biblical access system for Catholic theological and apologetics work.

## Cross References

Many passages belong to more than one doctrinal or apologetics topic. This index should therefore preserve strong cross-referencing across categories.

Examples of cross-reference patterns include:

- The Trinity ↔ Jesus Christ ↔ Holy Spirit
- The Church ↔ Authority ↔ Apostolic Succession ↔ The Papacy
- The Eucharist ↔ Sacrifice ↔ Passover ↔ New Covenant
- Grace ↔ Faith ↔ Works ↔ Justification ↔ Salvation
- Mary ↔ Incarnation ↔ New Eve ↔ Saints
- Kingdom of God ↔ The Church ↔ Covenants ↔ Israel ↔ New Covenant

Cross references should help contributors locate the full theological network surrounding a passage rather than isolating it within a single use.

## Future Expansion

This index should continue growing as Catholic Veritas expands.

Future development should:

- deepen existing doctrinal categories before multiplying new ones
- add more precise subtopics under major headings
- improve cross-references between doctrines and debates
- identify passages that repeatedly appear in apologetics work
- track passages that require careful contextual or language analysis
- strengthen the distinction between primary and supporting biblical evidence

Over time, this document should become a highly structured Scripture access system for Catholic apologetics, theology, and biblical study. Its long-term value will come from careful organization, consistency of topic structure, and disciplined expansion rather than from sheer volume alone.
