# Content Index

## Catholic Veritas

This document is the master content inventory of Catholic Veritas. It serves as the authoritative catalogue of every article, category, doctrine page, resource page, and planned page within the website.

Its purpose is to prevent duplicate content, preserve consistency, identify content gaps, organize future development, and support long-term maintainability as the project expands.

Every future AI contributor or human contributor must consult this file before proposing new pages, restructuring existing content, merging related topics, or redefining page scope.

## Overview

The Content Index exists to provide a single authoritative reference for the current and planned structure of Catholic Veritas.

This document should always reflect the current structure of the website as accurately as possible. Every article, whether published or still planned, should have a clearly defined place here.

Every new article should be added to this index immediately after creation. Every significant change to existing content should be reflected here as part of normal maintenance.

This document is not merely a list. It is a governance tool for preserving structural clarity and preventing drift across a large and growing knowledge base.

## Content Status Definitions

The following status values should be used consistently throughout this document.

### Planned

The page has been identified as useful and has an intended place in the site architecture, but no draft has yet been written.

### Draft

The page exists in written form but is incomplete, unreviewed, or not yet ready for formal publication.

### In Progress

The page is actively being developed, expanded, or significantly reworked.

### Published

The page is live and considered complete enough for public use under current standards.

### Needs Review

The page exists but should be checked for doctrinal accuracy, historical reliability, editorial consistency, broken links, or architectural fit.

### Needs Expansion

The page is published or drafted but remains too thin, too limited in scope, or incomplete relative to its long-term role.

### Archived

The page is no longer part of active development or public structure but is preserved for reference or internal record.

### Merged

The page’s content has been absorbed into another page and should no longer be treated as a separate active entry.

### Redirected

The page title or URL is no longer primary, and readers should be directed to another canonical page.

Pages on biblical canon, interpretation, biblical evidence, difficult passages, and the Catholic understanding of Scripture.

Pages on sacramental theology, the seven sacraments, the Mass, and related doctrinal questions.

Pages on the historical development of the Church, major eras, controversies, councils, and continuity across time.

Pages focused on patristic figures, writings, historical witness, and the role of the Fathers in Catholic continuity.

Pages devoted to explaining, defending, and clarifying Catholic teaching in response to objections or misunderstandings.

Pages on prayer, spiritual practice, devotions, contemplation, and the role of prayer in Catholic life.

Pages on conscience, sin, virtue, human dignity, Catholic ethics, and moral theology.

## Duplicate Detection

Before creating a new page, contributors should verify:

- Does a page already answer this question?
- Can the existing page be expanded?
- Would a subsection be sufficient?
- Would a redirect be more appropriate?

Duplicate content should generally be merged rather than maintained separately.

### Duplicate prevention rules

- Each primary question should have one canonical page.
- Each major doctrine should have one principal doctrinal home.
- Question pages should not duplicate full doctrinal pages unnecessarily.
- Objection pages should answer objections from a distinct angle rather than recreating the same article under another title.
- Slightly different phrasings of the same question should usually point to one canonical page through redirects, not separate parallel content.

If two pages overlap substantially, contributors should merge them, redefine their scope, or designate one as the canonical page and redirect the other.

## Future Expansion

Catholic Veritas is intended to scale to several thousand articles. This index should support that growth by preserving stable categories, clear parent-child relationships, and one canonical home for each topic.

Future expansion should:

- deepen existing categories before creating new top-level categories
- strengthen parent pages and child-page relationships
- clarify scope boundaries between similar pages
- identify gaps in foundational coverage
- maintain a stable hierarchy across time

Growth should strengthen the existing structure rather than repeatedly reinvent it. New complexity should be absorbed through better organization, not through unnecessary category proliferation.

## Maintenance Workflow

`CONTENT_INDEX.md` should be updated whenever an article is:

- created
- renamed
- merged
- deleted
- redirected
- significantly revised

### Maintenance rules

- Add new pages immediately after creation.
- Update status whenever a page moves from planning to drafting, publication, review, or archival.
- Update URLs and parent-page relationships whenever structural changes occur.
- Record mergers and redirects clearly so future contributors understand what happened.
- Keep notes concise but useful enough to explain unusual scope decisions or status changes.

This document should be treated as a living master inventory. If it falls out of sync with the actual structure of Catholic Veritas, duplication, inconsistency, and project drift become far more likely.
