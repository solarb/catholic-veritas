# Article Specification

## Catholic Veritas

This document defines the planning process that must occur before writing any article for Catholic Veritas. Every article, whether created by a human contributor or an AI contributor, should first have an Article Specification completed.

The purpose of this process is to eliminate scope creep, avoid duplicate content, preserve consistency, and ensure that every article contributes meaningfully to the overall Catholic Veritas knowledge base. This document is intended to become a permanent part of the editorial workflow.

Planning must always precede writing. No article should be written without first defining its objective, scope, sources, and relationship to the rest of Catholic Veritas.

## Purpose

Article planning is essential because Catholic Veritas is a long-term knowledge base rather than a collection of isolated pieces of writing. Without clear planning, articles tend to drift in scope, duplicate existing material, omit necessary evidence, or fail to integrate properly into the broader structure of the site.

The goal of the Article Specification is to ensure that every article has:

- a clear objective
- defined boundaries
- consistent structure
- proper integration into Catholic Veritas

This process protects the long-term coherence of the knowledge base and makes both writing and revision more disciplined.

## Article Identity

Every article specification should define the article’s identity within the structure of Catholic Veritas.

Required fields:

- Article Title
- URL Slug
- Category
- Parent Page
- Child Pages
- Related Articles

### Field guidance

**Article Title**

The planned title of the article. It should be clear, descriptive, and consistent with the naming conventions of Catholic Veritas.

**URL Slug**

The planned URL path or slug. It should reflect the article’s canonical place within the architecture.

**Category**

The top-level knowledge domain to which the article belongs.

**Parent Page**

The broader page under which the article logically sits, if applicable.

**Child Pages**

Any narrower subjects that should eventually branch from this article rather than remain inside it as oversized subsections.

**Related Articles**

Other articles that are conceptually adjacent and should be considered during planning to avoid overlap and strengthen internal linking.

## Primary Objective

Every article specification must include this statement:

**In one sentence, what is this article trying to prove, explain, or answer?**

Every article should have only one primary objective.

This requirement exists to prevent articles from trying to perform too many unrelated tasks at once. A page may contain supporting material, but it must have one controlling purpose that governs scope, structure, evidence, and internal linking.

If the objective cannot be stated clearly in one sentence, the article is not yet sufficiently defined.

## Target Audience

Every article specification should identify the intended readership.

Possible audience labels include:

- Beginner
- Catholic
- Protestant
- Orthodox
- Muslim
- Atheist
- Researcher
- RCIA Candidate

Multiple audiences may be selected when appropriate, but the article should still be planned with a clear sense of its primary reading context.

Audience definition affects:

- explanatory depth
- terminology
- background assumptions
- objection handling
- cross-link strategy

## Scope

Every article specification must define the scope of the article clearly before writing begins.

This should include:

- Included Topics
- Excluded Topics
- Topics that belong in separate articles

### Included Topics

List the themes, claims, passages, doctrines, or historical questions that the article is expected to cover directly.

### Excluded Topics

List the nearby or related issues that may arise naturally but should not be treated as part of the article’s central scope.

### Topics that belong in separate articles

List any subtopics that are important but large enough to deserve their own article rather than being absorbed into the planned page.

Scope definition exists to keep the article focused and to protect the architecture from disorder.

## Evidence Required

Every article specification should identify which kinds of evidence will be required before writing begins.

Possible evidence types include:

- Scripture
- Sacred Tradition
- Catechism
- Church Fathers
- Councils
- Vatican Documents
- Historical Sources
- Original Languages
- Archaeology
- Catholic Scholarship

Not every article requires every evidence type. The specification should identify what is necessary for the specific objective of the article.

This step ensures that the writer knows from the start what kind of research must be completed before drafting can responsibly begin.

## Common Objections

Every article specification should identify the major objections the article should address, if objections are relevant to the topic.

This section should list:

- biblical objections
- doctrinal objections
- historical objections
- apologetics misunderstandings
- common reader confusions

The goal is not to force every article into a debate format, but to make sure controversial or contested subjects are planned with full awareness of the strongest opposing arguments.

## Internal Linking

Every article specification should identify how the page will connect to the rest of Catholic Veritas.

This section should include:

- Prerequisite Articles
- Related Articles
- Recommended Next Reading

### Prerequisite Articles

Pages readers may need in order to understand the topic fully.

### Related Articles

Pages that are conceptually adjacent and should be linked naturally within or around the article.

### Recommended Next Reading

Pages that readers should logically move to after completing the article.

Planning internal links in advance helps prevent duplication and strengthens the overall knowledge base structure.

## Visual Resources

Every article specification should determine whether the article requires any visual aids.

Possible visual resources include:

- timeline
- map
- diagram
- comparison table
- infographic
- genealogy
- chart

The purpose of this section is to identify where a visual resource would materially improve understanding rather than merely decorate the page.

If no visual aid is necessary, that should be stated clearly.

## Completion Checklist

Before writing begins, the following should be verified:

- Objective is clear.
- Scope is defined.
- Duplicate articles do not already exist.
- Sources have been identified.
- Internal links are planned.
- Required visual aids are identified.

If any of these conditions remain unresolved, the article specification is not complete and drafting should not begin.

## Long-Term Maintenance

The Article Specification should be updated whenever the article undergoes significant revision.

Examples of revision that should trigger an update include:

- change of title
- change of URL slug
- change of primary objective
- major scope expansion or reduction
- reassignment to a different category or parent page
- major changes to evidence requirements
- substantial revision of objections or internal link strategy

The specification should remain aligned with the current purpose and structure of the article. If the article changes but the specification does not, future contributors are more likely to misread its intent, duplicate content, or weaken its architectural fit.
